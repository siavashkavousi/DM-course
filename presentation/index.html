Time Series Patterns

  • Trend
  • Seasonal
  • Cyclical
  • Random

Trend

a long term pattern
Trend, 10, 30, 20, 40, 30, 50, 40
value at time t which is regressed on a time trend
$Y_t = α + βt + ε_t$
  • long term relatively smooth pattern that usually persists for more than one year
  • varying mean over time

Seasonal

a pattern in a frequency of occurrence -> within a year or even shorter
  • frequency of occurrence is within a year or even shorter
  • A seasonal pattern exists when a series is influenced by seasonal factors (e.g., the quarter of the year, the month, or day of the week). Seasonality is always of a fixed and known perio
Seasonal, 20, 45, 35, 90, 20, 60, 45, 80, 20, 40, 30, 75, 10, 70, 60, 90, 20

Cyclical

like seasonal a repeated pattern but beyond a frequency of one year
Cyclical, 20, 90, 30, 50, 35, 80, 10, 75, 10

Random

no particular pattern
value at time t is regressed on the last period's value
$Y_t$ = last period value + error(noise)
$Y_t = Y_{t-1} + ε_t$
  • non mean reverting process that can move away from the mean either in a positive or negative direction
  • variance evolves over time and goes to infinity as time goes to infinity
  • cannot be predicted
  • Is a white noise, which means εt is independent and identically distributed with mean "0" and variance "σ²".

Different Time Series Processes

  • White Noise
  • AutoRegressive Model
  • Moving Average Model
  • AutoRegressive Moving Average Model

White Noise

It is purely random

AutoRegressive Model

An AR model is one in which Yt depends only on its own past values Yt-1, Yt-2, Yt-3, etc

AR(p): Yt = Β0 + Β1 Yt-1 + Β2 Yt-2 + ... + Βp Yt-p

Moving Average Model

A technique to remove the fine-grained variation between time steps

The hope of smoothing is to remove noise and better expose the signal of the underlying causal processes

AutoRegressive Moving Average Model

There are situations where the time series may be represented as a mix of both AR and MA models referred as ARMA
